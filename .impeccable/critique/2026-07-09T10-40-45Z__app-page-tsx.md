## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment: PASS.** Distinctive signature (intro monogram → dye-on-dock hero), committed pink, real portrait cutout, warm first-person copy ("building this site, in the open"). Not template-shaped.

**Deterministic scan**: CLI detector: 0 findings across app/components/lib. In-page detector (all 4 routes): flagged the Send Message button and nav CTA at 4.4:1 (#181F1C on #DB5461) — a real catch the code audit missed; ~103ch line length on right-column card prose — real; "body text touching viewport edge" ×4 — false positives (right column measured while translated off-screen pre-collapse); "positioned child clipped by overflow container" — plausible false positive (fixed scroll cue escapes overflow-x-clip in practice); "overused font: Inter" — accepted brand identity, not actionable.

**Visual overlays**: headless browser only, so no user-visible overlay tab; findings captured via the in-page detector API instead.

## Overall Impression

The opening 10 seconds are the best thing here: intro monogram, then the figure-ground inversion as the hero dyes pink. It reads as engineered, not decorated, which is exactly the brief. The weaknesses are at the edges: the docked hero geometry breaks on short laptop screens, and the end of the funnel (/contact) is the least considered screen on the site.

## What's Working

1. **The dye-on-dock signature**: at rest the top state is quiet carbon with one pink square; scrolling inverts figure and ground until the docked card is the page's single loud object. Self-correcting, reduced-motion safe, and genuinely memorable.
2. **The timeline**: curated Overview default, jobs under Career, story-first copy ("My first real software job"), one consistent control language shared with /projects.
3. **Material discipline**: one surface recipe (tone + inset highlight + shadow, no borders) across nav, cards, chips, and dropdowns. Nothing competes with the pink.

## Priority Issues

1. **[P1] Docked hero clips the "Currently…" line behind the buttons on short viewports.** At 1280×800 and 1366×768 (very common work laptops) the docked content stack (portrait 312 + headline ~235 + buttons ~90) exceeds the card's content height (~616 at 800px vh), and `buttonsTopV` lerps to the card floor at dock=1 with no clamp against the headline bottom. The overlap is visible in the settled state, not just mid-transition. **Fix**: clamp the buttons' docked position below the headline bottom at every dock value, and shrink the docked portrait/text scale when `vh` is short so the stack fits. **Command**: /impeccable adapt.
2. **[P1] Ink-on-pink CTAs use the wrong ink and miss AA.** Send Message (ContactForm) and the nav "Let's talk" use `#181F1C` on `#DB5461` = 4.36:1 (needs 4.5). The design system already solves this: `heroInk #10120F` = 4.89:1. Two one-line token swaps that also enforce the documented "never grey/bg ink on pink" rule. **Command**: /impeccable polish.
3. **[P2] Card prose has no line-length cap.** About Me paragraph runs ~103 chars/line at 1280 and wider on 1440+ (right column is ~720-900px). Cap prose at ~70ch inside SectionCard/ProjectCard bodies. **Command**: /impeccable typeset.
4. **[P2] /contact is a dead end if the form fails, and the sparsest page on the site.** No direct email anywhere, no socials on the page; a recruiter who prefers their mail client has no path, and an EmailJS outage leaves "please try again" as the only recovery. Add a direct email line + GitHub/LinkedIn under the form. This is also the end of the funnel: peak-end rule says the last screen deserves more than it currently gets. **Command**: /impeccable harden.
5. **[P3] The intro can't be skipped.** ~3s scroll-locked on first visit each session, pointer-events disabled. A click/Esc skip that jumps to the glide would respect impatient visitors without losing the signature. **Command**: /impeccable animate.

## Persona Red Flags

And she can't verify the two hackathon projects: Skool Swaps and Logic Lift have `links: {}`, screenshots only. If repos exist, link them; unverifiable claims are discounted.

**Alex (impatient power user)**: forced ~3s intro with scroll lock, no skip on click/Esc. Everything after is fast (deep links, keyboard, one-at-a-time accordions).

**Casey (distracted mobile user)**: static pink card loads fast, touch targets now compliant. The nav is a top dock, out of the one-handed thumb zone; acceptable for a 4-item portfolio nav, but the "Let's talk" CTA lives at the hardest-to-reach corner on mobile.

**Sam (screen reader / keyboard)**: strong post-harden (labels, focus trap, listbox semantics, focus rings, reduced motion). Remaining: the two 4.4:1 pink CTAs (issue 2).

## Minor Observations

- "Get in Touch" (home section) vs "Let's talk" (nav CTA + /contact h1): pick one phrase for the same action.
- Hover-pink text on tone surfaces (featured titles 2.69:1, course chips 4.36:1) is sub-AA in the hover state only; resting states pass and motion cues accompany. Judgment call, documented previously.
- The portrait renders ~1-2s late on a cold load (image decode); `priority` is already set, so this is likely just dev-server latency. Worth one check on the production deploy.
- Home hero big-state at 800px-tall viewports is handsome and correct; only the docked state overflows (issue 1).

## Questions to Consider

- Your signature survives every viewport you develop on; who is checking it on the 768px-tall ThinkPad a recruiter actually uses?
- The most persuaded visitor on the site is the one who clicks "Let's talk". Should the funnel end on the sparsest page?
- If EmailJS is down the day a Jane Street recruiter writes to you, where does that message go?
