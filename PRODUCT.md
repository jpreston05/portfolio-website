# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and engineers from quant trading firms (Optiver, Jane Street, IMC, Citadel) and
Atlassian, screening a software engineering student's portfolio. They skim fast, have seen
hundreds of templated portfolios, and reward substance and taste over decoration.

## Product Purpose

Jack Preston's single-page-first personal portfolio (Next.js, multipage: home, /projects,
/timeline, /contact). It exists to get interviews: show engineering judgment through the site
itself. Success = a reviewer remembers it and reads past the first fold.

## Brand Personality

Memorable through competence. Quiet, precise, confident; personality carried by one signature
move (the branded intro → collapsing hero card) rather than ornament. Warm first-person copy,
no corporate voice, no em dashes in prose.

## Anti-references

Templated AI portfolios: blue/purple gradients, glowing text, glassmorphism, AI/stock hero
imagery, playful illustration, generic "Developer/Designer" cycling text, numbered section
eyebrows. Anything that competes with the one loud pink object.

## Design Principles

- One loud object: the pink hero card is the only committed color moment; everything else stays
  quiet carbon monochrome, with pink reserved for interactive/active states.
- No card borders: surfaces separate by tone, inset top-highlight, and soft shadow; hairlines
  are for dividers only.
- The signature must be genuinely smooth and self-correcting (intro loader → hero collapse),
  never a static screenshot.
- Restraint everywhere else; substance over decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA is non-negotiable (Jack studied accessibility). Verified contrast pairs documented in
CLAUDE.md; muted grey `#A6B0A8` passes on both surfaces, `#737F77` is decorative-only. Full
keyboard operability, visible focus (accent ring, ink on the pink card), semantic HTML,
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, non-color cues alongside any
red/green status.
